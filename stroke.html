shoshin

One stroke.

A stroke has a beginning and an end. Between them, it carries energy. The brush does not hesitate. It does not plan. It moves — and in its movement, one of five qualities appears.

Earth —
heavy, grounded, slow. The stroke presses into the paper. Horizontal. Stable.
Water —
flowing, continuous, yielding. The stroke moves without breaking. Curves. No sharp edges.
Fire —
ascending, quick, tapering. The stroke rises and thins. Vertical. Decisive.
Wind —
light, sweeping, unpredictable. The stroke lifts from the surface. Diagonal. Free.
Sky —
the space the stroke moves through. What is not drawn is also the calligraphy.

Traditional forms to practice: the circle (ensō), bamboo (竹), the horizontal stroke (一), the mountain (山), the dot (丶). Each contains all five elements. Begin with one stroke. That is enough.

Make one stroke with full attention.
Do not evaluate. Make another.
Each is complete.
